# Quantitative Biology and Computational Modeling

This repository contains the mathematical models, numerical simulations, and theoretical documentation developed during the Biomathematics course. The project focuses on the rigorous application of differential equations to biological systems, ranging from molecular kinetics to electrophysiology.

---

##  Enzyme Kinetics and Reaction Dynamics
This section covers the fundamental principles of biochemical reactions and their mathematical representations.

* **Mass Action Law:** Foundational models for chemical kinetics and two-stage enzymatic reactions.
* **Steady-State Approximations:** Implementation of Quasi-Steady State Approximation (QSSA) and thermodynamic equilibrium.
* **Inhibition and Cooperativity:** Analysis of substrate inhibition, competitive inhibition, and molecular cooperativity.
* **Asymptotic Analysis:** Construction of uniform approximations, non-dimensionalization, and boundary layer analysis.

## Electrophysiology and Membrane Biophysics
Detailed documentation on the electrical properties of biological membranes and signal propagation.

* **Ionic Transport:** Nernst-Planck equation and the Goldman-Hodgkin-Katz (GHK) current-voltage relationship.
* **Action Potential Models:** Full implementation of the Hodgkin-Huxley model (0D and 1D) including gating variables.
* **Reduced Systems:** Analysis of the FitzHugh-Nagumo model and the Luo-Rudy I cardiac model.
* **Wave Propagation:** Cable equation, Traveling Waves, and Kolmogorov-Fisher-Nagumo systems.

##  Numerical Methods and Laboratorial Simulations
Computational tools used to solve the biological models described above.

* **ODE Solvers:** Application of specialized algorithms for stiff biological systems (e.g., `ode15s`).
* **PDE Problems:** Numerical solutions for 1D elliptic problems using Finite Difference Methods.
* **Diffusion:** Numerical approximation of the 1D Heat Equation in biological contexts.

---

## Repository Structure
The files are organized to reflect the chronological and thematic progression of the course:

* **Lectures/**: Theoretical notes and asymptotic analysis of biological models.
* **Labs/**: MATLAB and Python scripts for numerical simulations and data visualization.
* **Exams/**: Solutions and analysis for past examination papers.
